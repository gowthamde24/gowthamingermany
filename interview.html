The Visa Interview 🎤

Common questions asked by the Visa Officer (VO) and how to answer them.

The Wrong Answer: "Because it's free education and I love cars."

The Right Approach: Focus on the quality of education, research facilities, and the specific curriculum of your course. Mention that German degrees are practical-oriented.

"Germany is a global leader in [Your Field, e.g., Mechanical Engineering]. The curriculum at [Your Uni] offers a specific module on [Subject] which aligns perfectly with my career goals..."

Do not say "Because I got admission here." You must show you researched the university.

  • Mention a specific Professor's work.
  • Mention a specific lab or research center there.
  • Mention the course modules that interest you.

Avoid: "I want to settle in Germany forever." (This can sound like immigration intent, which VOs sometimes dislike).

"My goal is to become an expert in [Field]. After my Masters, I plan to gain some international work experience in the German industry for a few years to apply what I've learned, and eventually contribute to the sector in India/Globally."

Be very clear about your finances. The VO wants to know you won't run out of money.

"My father is sponsoring me. He is a [Job Title] with an annual income of [Amount]. Additionally, we have secured an education loan of [Amount] from [Bank] and I have already deposited €11,208 in a Blocked Account."
